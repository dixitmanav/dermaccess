Disability Access Is
Dermatologic Care

Dermatology depends on seeing, communicating, touching, positioning, photographing, explaining, and following up. For patients with disabilities, each of these steps can create barriers when clinics are not designed with accessibility in mind. DermAccess is an educational resource focused on disability-inclusive dermatology care, with practical tools for patients, caregivers, students, and clinicians.

For Patients & Caregivers For Clinicians
Why This Matters

Access goes far beyond ramps and parking spaces

Disability access in dermatology includes communication for Deaf and hard-of-hearing patients, screen-reader-friendly portals for blind and low-vision patients, adjustable exam tables for patients with mobility disabilities, and sensory-aware visits for patients with developmental disabilities. Dermatology care is not fully accessible until every patient can communicate, understand, and participate meaningfully in their own care.

Legal Foundation

Under ADA effective communication rules, covered entities must provide auxiliary aids and services when needed so that people with vision, hearing, or speech disabilities can communicate effectively — receiving information from and conveying information to the entity. Access is a legal obligation, not just a clinical courtesy.

About This Project

A student-led initiative in dermatology access

M
Mihir Shah
Medical Student — Dermatology & Health Equity

I created this project as a medical student interested in dermatology, disability access, and health equity. My goal is to translate existing accessibility guidance and dermatology literature into practical, respectful, and patient-centered resources. DermAccess aims to support patients in navigating the healthcare system and to help clinics think more intentionally about inclusive care — without overclaiming or providing clinical or legal directives.
