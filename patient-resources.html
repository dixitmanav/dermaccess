Patient & Caregiver Resource Library

This resource library compiles publicly available information relevant to disability access, communication, dermatology visits, and skin health education. Resources are included for educational purposes and do not replace individualized medical, legal, or clinical advice.

Disability Rights & Communication Access

Disability Rights

ADA Effective Communication

The primary official source for communication access under the ADA. Explains the purpose of effective communication rules and the role of auxiliary aids and services for people with hearing, vision, and speech disabilities.

ADA.gov
Disability Rights

ADA Service Animals

ADA guidance on service animals in public accommodations, including healthcare settings. The ADA does not require service animals to wear a vest, ID tag, or specific harness. Clinics may only ask two questions.

ADA.gov — Service Animals
Disability Rights

HHS Disability Rights / Section 504

HHS guidance on disability nondiscrimination in healthcare, including accessible medical diagnostic equipment and protections under Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act for recipients of federal funding.

HHS Civil Rights

Dermatology Patient Education

Dermatology

AAD Skin Self-Exam Resources

The American Academy of Dermatology provides patient education on checking the skin and recommends seeing a board-certified dermatologist for spots that are different from others or that change, itch, or bleed.

AAD.org — Skin Self-Exam
Dermatology

AAD Eczema Resources

The AAD provides dermatologist-reviewed information about eczema types, triggers, and when it can be helpful to see a board-certified dermatologist for diagnosis and management.

AAD.org — Eczema
Dermatology

AAD Public Education Library

The AAD's public education section includes information on skin, hair, and nail conditions, everyday care, cosmetic treatments, and parent and child resources for a wide range of dermatologic topics.

AAD.org — Patient Education

Web Accessibility

Web Accessibility

WCAG 2.2

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines define how to make web content more accessible for people with disabilities, including visual, auditory, physical, speech, cognitive, language, learning, and neurologic disabilities. WCAG 2.2 is the current standard.

W3C — WCAG 2.2
